Richelle Choy

Although I have taken PLTW Robotics in eighth grade, Computer Science Principles is my first and last engineering class that I have taken throughout my high school career. After learning the basics of applications such as Photoshop and Autodesk Maya, I became more intrigued by computers and other electronics, influencing me to take the Computer Science Principles course offered at Dublin High School. I am looking forward to expanding my knowledge and comprehension of Computer Science in college.

In addition to my interest in Computer Science, I also am fascinated by the artistic world. Every single year in high school, I have enrolled in a visual arts class, ranging from the typical traditional art to three-dimensional modelling. My volunteer experience at the Children's Creativity Museum in San Francisco allowed me to share these passions by teaching children and adults the fundamentals of stop-motion animation and basic coding. Art has always been my creative outlet, and I hope Computer Science will be one as well.

As the former secretary of the Stress Free Club in Dublin High School, I realized that responsibility was crucial to the fulfillment of the role. My duties within the club include sending notifications through email and Remind for meetings, responding to questions asked by members, preparing activities, and recording the duration, number of participants, and activities of meetings.

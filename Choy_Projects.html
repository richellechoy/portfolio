Project: Scratch Triathlon Game
Course: Computer Science Principles
Duration: 8/30/18 - 9/6/18
Objective: To Create An Interactive Game
Type of Project: Collaborative

The objective of this project was to design an interactive game that consisted of levels with varying difficulty that made players feel a sense of progression. My partner and I collectively agreed that it was appropriate to design the game based off a triathlon, in which three levels are dedicated to running, biking, and swimming respectively. As the player progresses further into the game, they will encounter more obstacles that will prevent them from reaching the finish line. In order to be qualified to pass the finish line, the player must collect three stars that are scattered throughout the game. Because this game restarts from the beginning when the player comes into contact with an obstacle, it encourages players to apply their experiences from their mistakes to progress further in the game, thus developing their skill. My role during the creation process of this game alternated betweeen being a leader and a follower. Sometimes I would be the individual would be making suggestions, and other times I would be the individual that is directly altering and improving the code. Despite the successes that we experienced, we also encountered issues that hindered our process. Initially, we decided to implement a jumping system, similar to many popular platforming games. However, the jumping movement was not as smooth as expected, thus negatively impacting the player's sense of fun. In order to replace the jumping system, we decided to have players avoid obstacles by using the arrow keys.

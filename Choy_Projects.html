Project: Scratch Triathlon Game
Course: Computer Science Principles
Duration: 8/30/18 - 9/6/18
Objective: To Create An Interactive Game
Type of Project: Collaborative

The objective of this project was to design an interactive game that consisted of levels with varying difficulty that made players feel a sense of progression. My partner and I collectively agreed that it was appropriate to design the game based off a triathlon, in which three levels are dedicated to running, biking, and swimming respectively.

As the player progresses further into the game, they will encounter more obstacles that will prevent them from reaching the finish line. In order to be qualified to pass the finish line, the player must collect three stars that are scattered throughout the game. Since this game restarts from the beginning when the player comes into contact with an obstacle, it encourages players to apply their experiences from their mistakes to progress further in the game, thus developing their skill.

My role during the creation process of this game alternated betweeen being a leader and a follower. Sometimes I would be the individual making suggestions, and other times I would be the individual that is directly altering and improving the code. Despite the successes that we experienced, we also encountered issues that hindered our process. Initially, we decided to implement a jumping system, similar to many popular platforming games. However, the jumping movement was not as smooth as expected, thus negatively impacting the player's sense of fun. In order to replace the jumping system, we decided to have players avoid obstacles by using the arrow keys.

Project: Image Manipulation
Course: Computer Science Principles
Duration: 2/25/19-
Objective: To Create a Program To Manipulate Images for a Client
Type of Project: Collaborative

The objective of this project was to design a program that will satisfy the image manipulation requirements for a client, which was the World Wildlife Fund. We were tasked to incorporate a consistent logo, a geometric shaped border, abstract act, and a range of options to cusotmize their image.

In order to operate the program, an individual must upload an image (must be .jpg) to the 1.4.7 folder. Then, type in %run 1.4.7/Final.py in the code editor and press enter. Next, type in modify('whatever') and replace 'whatever' with 'nice', 'warm', 'cool', 'neutral', 'logo_and_border_1', or 'logo_and_border_2'. The first four options perform pixel manipulation while the last two adds a logo and border onto the desired image. Three equivalent colored stripes with a black silhouette of the image will be produced as a result.

My role in this particular project was primarily to code. It was crucial to communicate errors and ideas to my partner, which we worked to implement and fix together. While experimenting with image fr manipulation, we encountered an issue. When specifying ranges for the colored stripes, we realized that it would only apply for image with 2000x3000 pixels. However, we resolved this issue by establishing two different variables that we can implement into our code multiples times and divide them by three in order to create three equivalent stripes on images, regardless of pixel size.
